Fats

You may be surprised to learn that fat is an important part of your diet. It's true that some fats are very bad for you, however some are also good for you. The difference is whether the fat is saturated (bad) or unsaturated (good)

Bad Fats (Avoid these)

These are fats that are high in saturated fats and should be avoided where possible. In addition to these two plant-based examples, pretty much all animal products (buttter, meat etc) are high in saturated fats so congratulations you are already avoiding them.

Palm and Palm Kernel Oil:
Palm Oil is 51% saturated fat, and Palm Kernel Oil is 86% saturated fat. Palm oils also have negative impact on animal habitats due to deforestation

Coconut Oil:
Coconut Oil is 87% saturated fat and should only be consumed in extreme moderation

Good Fats (Eat plenty of these)

These are fats that are low in saturated fats compared to unsaturated fats and are an integral part of a healthy diet.

Avocado:
Only 14% of the fat in an Avocado is saturated, and 75% of their calories comes from fat making it one of the richest sources of unsaturated fats.

Flax Seeds AKA Linseed:
Flax seeds are 41% fat, only 8% of which is saturated making it an excellent source of unsaturated fat.
