Carbohydrates

You may have heard of low-carb diets as being a good thing, and while there is some merit to that as most diets have an excess of carbohydrate and a deficiency in protein, fibre and fat, the ideal is a balance of each. It is important, however, to ensure that the carbohydrates in your diet are complex carbohydrates that provide energy gradually over time rather than simple carbs such as sugar that release all of the energy up front.

Complex Carbs (Eat these)

Wholemeal bread:
Wholegrain bread contains complex carbs and is a good source of your daily carbohydrate intake

Brown Rice:
Brown rice is also a complex carb and good to eat plenty of (choose brown rice over white)

Simple Carbs (Avoid these)

White bread:
White bread, while still high in carbohydrate, is made of mostly simple carbs that are processed by your body in a similar way to sugar.

Sugars:
Sugar is one of the worst things to eat, as many are already aware. However, be careful as sugar is in a lot of things you might not expect, for example fruit juices and other processed foods.
